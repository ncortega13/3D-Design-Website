Introduction to AutoCAD

AutoCAD is an abbreviation for Auto Computer-Aided Design. It is a commercial design and drafting software used in 2D and 3D design. John Walker is the founder of the Autodesk Company, which offers a software application for digitally designing, drafting, and modeling all types of structures and objects. AutoCAD was released in December 1982 by the Autodesk Company as a desktop application that ran on microcontrollers with internal graphic controllers.


Surveyors, designers, engineers, and drafters utilize AutoCAD all around the world. AutoCAD has appeared to be a reliable and user-friendly program on a global scale. AutoCAD is a computer-aided design (CAD) and drafting software product. AutoCAD software is well-known worldwide for its exceptional editing capabilities, making it possible to draft construction blueprints and produce 3D images digitally.

Some Characteristics of AutoCAD
  • AutoCAD is a worldwide program. Product development teams, industrial facilities, medical professionals, educational institutions, professionals, and engineers use it worldwide.
  • The program's two most significant features are 3D modeling and visualization. Using numerous 3D tools and commands, modelers may generate advanced 3D models, wireframes, meshes, and surfaces in AutoCAD.
  • The ability to build section planes is available in AutoCAD. These section planes aid in the creation of cross-sectional images of three-dimensional objects. To explore the inner features of 3D objects, users can edit, select, or move the section planes.
  • AutoCAD can also import 3D models from other CAD software. Designers can also work on maps or locations that have are available from the internet.
