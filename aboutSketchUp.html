Introduction to SketchUp

SketchUp is one of the most well-known and widely used 3D modeling programs available. It is available in both a free and a paid version, making it a viable alternative for both pros and enthusiasts. SketchUp is a useful software package that allows users to generate 2D and 3D models for a range of applications, including mechanical engineering, architecture, and even gaming. Of course, that's only the beginning; there's still a lot to learn about SketchUp.


SketchUp is an intuitive 3D modeling application that lets you create and edit 2D and 3D models with a patented “Push and Pull” method. The Push and Pull tool allows designers to extrude any flat surface into 3D shapes. All you have to do is click an object and then start pulling it until you like what you see (Gavin, 2018).

Features of SketchUp
  • 3D Modeling: The easiest method to develop, document, and express your 3D ideas.
  • Iterate in 3D: Develop your projects rapidly by working through your ideas in 3D space.
  • Accurate & Detailed Models: The importance of accuracy from the start cannot be overstated. In all stages of a project, SketchUp allows you to create, specify, and plan.
  • Extensibility: Make SketchUp exactly what you need it to be by using the Extension Warehouse.
  • Objects: With SketchUp's objects, you can work smart and fast
  • Customization: Make every project's style your own by customizing its look and feel.
Three Versions of SketchUp
  • SketchUp Free: All you need is a good internet connection to use this entirely free cloud-based service. It enables anyone to create a 3D model of their item using a range of tools. On the cloud, you'll be able to create lines, arcs, forms, and a variety of other items. There are other tools for painting, measurement, and displacement, allowing you to visualize any element.
  • SketchUp Shop: This edition includes more advanced features than the free version and was created for professionals looking for cost-effective 3D modeling solutions. It is designed for makers, inventors, and designers since it helps them to envision construction projects before they are built.
  • SketchUp Pro The software's most advanced solution for use on a computer. It is composed of three programs: Pro, LayOut, and Builder. SketchUp Pro is a more advanced version of SketchUp that is aimed for all professionals who require a complete modeling solution.
Hardware and Software Requirements

Windows 10, Windows 8+ and Windows 7+

  • Software
    • An internet connection is required to install and authorize SketchUp and to use some of the features.
    • Microsoft® Internet Explorer 9.0 or higher. We recommend Google Chrome.
    • SketchUp Pro requires .NET Framework version 4.5.2. SketchUp requires a 64-bit version of Windows. Also, to install SketchUp, Windows 8.1 must be current with Windows Update.
  • Recommended Hardware
    • 2+ GHz processor
    • 8+ GB RAM
    • 700MB of available hard-disk space
    • 3D class video card with 1 GB of memory or higher and support for hardware acceleration. Please ensure that the video card driver supports OpenGL 3.1 or higher and is up to date. SketchUp's performance relies heavily on the graphics card driver and its ability to support OpenGL 3.1 or higher.
    • 3-button, scroll-wheel mouse.
  • Minimum Hardware
    • 1 GHz processor
    • 4GB RAM
    • 500MB of free hard-disk space
    • 3D class video card with 512 MB of memory or higher and support for hardware acceleration. Please ensure that the video card driver supports OpenGL 3.1 or higher and is up to date.

    • 3D class video card with 1 GB of memory or higher and support for hardware acceleration. Please ensure that the video card driver supports OpenGL version 3.1 or higher and is up to date.
    • 3-button, scroll-wheel mouse.
  • Minimum Hardware
    • 2.1+ GHz Intel™ processor
    • 4GB RAM
    • 500MB of available hard-disk space.
    • 3D class video card with 512 MB of memory or higher and support for hardware acceleration. Please ensure that the video card driver supports OpenGL version 3.1 or higher and up to date.
    • 3-button, scroll-wheel mouse.
